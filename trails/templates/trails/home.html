Trail Tips — From Me to You

  • Start early: Trails can take longer than expected — and some parts can be tricky in late afternoon light.
  • Stay aware: Take short breaks to stay focused, especially on longer or steeper trails. Reassess your pace and your plan as you go — it’s okay to change direction.
  • Water, always: Even if it's cool outside, bring enough water. Some trails don’t have fountains along the way.
  • Know your limits: Choose hikes that match your energy for the day — not just what looks pretty.
  • Gear matters: Thunderstorms or fog can sneak up on you. Use sturdy hiking shoes with good grip to stay safe and comfortable. A light jacket and a rain cover are always a good idea.
  • Plan your route: Always allow extra time and be prepared with an alternative route if needed. Check trail conditions and weather before starting.
